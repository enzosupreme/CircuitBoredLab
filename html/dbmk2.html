DiceBoard Mk2

I was really proud of and often use my Diceboard , but I wanted to design and build one from scratch. I also needed an excuse to learn and practice designing with Fusion360. I wanted something simple and satisfying to hold as a handheld device. Thus I began work on DiceBoard Mk2.

Stage 1: Design

After stumbling through several youtube tutorials, I was able to get acclimated to Fusion360 enough to get started designing an enclosure for the new diceboard. I kept it simple by designing a small rectangular box with holes cut out for the usb port and power switch. I decided to try out a wide oled to save space. I measaured the OLED and the keyswitches to design a base plate for the components. I went for a six button set to cover a full set of dice plus an extra button to total dicerolls.

I chose white, glow-in-the-dark filament for the base of the enclosure and black for the plate. The prints worked perfectly and the overall process helped me build my confidence with Fusion360.

Stage 2: Build

The build was similar to the first one. I used a wide OLED this time, but all of the same components as the last build. It took no more than half an hour to wire and solder everything up. I used a hot glue gun to adhere the plate to the base. I used the same program I wrote for the first Diceboard with some edits for the display. As an added bonus I created a "holo display attatchment to give it a more sci-fi aesthetic."
