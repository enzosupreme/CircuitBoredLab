Multi-Hex

A portable, wearable device with tons of features. The idea behind this project was to create a platform for me to experiment and tinker with circuit python. I also wanted to get more experience designing PCBs.

Stage 1: Design and Build

I came up with a hexagonal board that houses a micro OLED display, 4 mini tactile switches, a CR2302 battery and a seeeduino micro-controller.

I wanted to make this wearable from the jump, so I came up with a design that would allow for embedded magnets to hold the hex board. I made the board with holes to screw into a base, but ended up glueing it to the base I came up with. I made a strap and a separate peg to clip them together and printed them with flexible filament. The flex material worked perfectly, the straps turned out sturdy but stretchy. I made the board to be purposely easy to assemble. It usually takes around 20 minutes to put one together, which is great because I plan on making 10 of this as christmas gifts this year.

Stage 2: Code

I didn't fully know what I wanted to write for this one when i first started, but there were a few things I did want to try. A few months ago I made a bot on discord called chip gizmo. Chip is basically a chatbot but has several features I wrote. He can provide suggestions on what to eat or watch and can answer yes or no questions among other things. The first thing I wanted to try was to make a device that did some of stuff I wrote for my discord bot. When I made the board I labelled the buttons A,B,C and D. The first version(module?) I made had a splash screen that showed the button mapping when you turned it on. Each button had a different function; Dice roller,YES or NO, What to eat and What to watch. This version was incredibly basic but was a nice start.

I soon made new version of the GM tool that has much better loot box and character creator. I also replaced the magropad.

I spent most of my thanksgiving break coming up with a menu and came up with what I consider a template. Version 2.0 lets you scroll up and down with A and B, D lets you select the highlighted feature and the C button goes back to the main menu.(A+B plays an animation I made). I plan on doing more with this. I'm working on being able to switch between full .py programs allowing for more functionality.
