CyberDeck

A cyberdeck is a computer you build in a non-conventional format sometimes made portable and wearable. I've been wanting to build a cyberdeck since I first saw one years ago, but never had an idea of where to start. After seeing Zack Freedman's build, I knew I wanted to make it wrist mounted. The wheels began turning and I came up with a no skill over the counter build.

Stage 1: Planning and Prep

I knew a raspberry pi was the way to pull this off. Its a small singleboard computer with tons of goodies that plug in, snap on or attach to in some other clever way. So I bought a raspberry pi, an HDMI capable rpi display, a mini bluetooth keyboard, a micro sd card and an altoid tin

Stage 2: Build

There isn't a lot to say about this build, the only part of this build you can't buy on amazon is the 3D printed phone Clamp. I use a modular phone clamp to hold the pi with the attached display. The base/clamp needed to be scaled to fit the altoid tin. I use the altoid tin to serve as a sort of base that the keyboard can lay on. I used magnetic top to adhere to the bottom of the keyboard to allow it to stick to the altoid tin. The altoid actually rests snuggly on a magnetic portable battery charger that I use to power on the pi.

Stage 3: Software

To setup the pi I used raspberry pi imager to install Manjaro linux (a flavor of arch linux). I used SSH to transfer files from my desktop to the pi (yes i could have used a flash drive, but I wanted to feel like a hackerman) and the setup was done.

Stage 4: Features

Not only can this thing carry curiously strong mints to keep your breath fresh, but you can swap differnt OSes by changing SD cards to do things like run Retropie. Swap the mints with an sd card holder in an altoid tin.

The cyberdeck can be attached to a portable battery pack that serves as a magnetic wrist mount while being housed in a cellphone wrist strap. The pi/display can easily slide out of the clamp and be re-mounted into something like a camera rig(see slides). The pi has usb ports so any keyboard, mouse or controller can be connected. At the moment I'm working on more altoid inserts, so more features to come.
