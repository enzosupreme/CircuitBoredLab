Green Lantern Power Battery and Ring

while experimenting with designing prints with embedded magnets, I came up with the idea of making a magnet activated prop. Making a green lantern power battery and ring seeming like a fun challenge.

Stage 1: Design

The idea was to design a lantern with leds that would be activated by using a magnet that would be detected by a hall effect sensor to trigger the leds in the lantern. The magnet would be embedded into the ring and the user would use the ring to light up the lantern. The Green Lantern has an icon design so I used some reference images to come up with a workable design in fusion 360.

After getting the basic shape down, I made holes to route the 10 green LEDs. I then made conical covers for the two side openings. I then designed a ring with a void for a small magnet. With parts ready it was time to construct and wire the lantern.

Stage 2: Build

I made holes in the front of the base lantern long enough to go through the back of it. I fed some pre-wired LEDs from the front to the back of the lantern where I would do all the wiring. I proceeded to wire up a hall sensor to a circuit python capable microcontroller and wrote a quick program that would turn on the lights after the hall sensor was triggered.
