Laser Pistol Mk1

Art creates art. I often look to visual art to find inspiration for project ideas. I recently finally got around to watching the movie, The Rocketeer. What I really loved about the movie was the design of the gadgets as well as the 1930s obsession with speed. I became a bit enamored by the dieselpunk style of the movie and went down a rabbithole of exploring the art style of Streamline Moderne. I found many artist renditions of crazy gadgets in the styles of dieselpunk, atompunk and raypunk when I came across props such as rayguns and laser pistols. I was immediately inspired to make one of my own.

Stage 1: Design and Build

The design was the first and most difficult challenge. I wanted to apply the principles of Streamline Moderne. Curves. Curves, Horizontality and a sense of motion. I used random drawings and paintings of raypunk rayguns and even ai generated examples for reference. Finally, I came up with something that looks vaguely like the planet express ship from futurama. I designed it as one piece initially and then proceded to cut it into parts and hallow them out in a crude fashion. I made holes for a button to trigger lights, sounds and a laser pointer when pressed, a power switch and usb port. I then printed all of the parts. The barrell and the surrounding fins were printed in phosphorescent filament. I wired three uv leds to a micro controller and fed them through the barrell, this would cause a glowing after effect after being triggered. I wired up the laser pointer module, batter pack, power switch and button and glued it all shut. To cover the glue and add a bit of flair, I later drew a stripe down the middle of the laser pistol with a 3d pen.

Stage 2: Code

There wasn't much to it, I just recorded a simple sound file with ableton and had it trigger after a button press the way I have in other projects. Sounds, lights and laser all trigger simultaneously.
